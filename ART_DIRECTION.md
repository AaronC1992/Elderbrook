# Elderbrook Art Direction (Code-Inferred)

This direction is inferred from the current implementation and content structure.

## Core Pillars

- **Classic browser RPG readability** inspired by AQ/DragonFable: strong silhouettes, clear enemy archetypes, and UI-forward presentation.
- **Dating-sim social layer**: character-forward scenes, expression-rich portrait framing, and cozy downtime moments between combat loops.
- **Warm gold UI language** over cool navy fantasy backgrounds to preserve visual identity and hierarchy.

## Tone + Mood

- Frontier-fantasy village under pressure (goblin raids, fading Wardstones).
- Day-cycle and season framing should support life-sim rhythm: morning / evening shifts with social opportunities.
- Combat scenes should feel dangerous but not grimdark; social scenes should feel intimate, hopeful, and slightly nostalgic.

## Visual Targets

- **Environment**: painterly backgrounds with depth layers, atmospheric fog, and readable POI areas.
- **Portraits**: chest-up character renders, expressive eyes/pose, neutral-to-warm skin tones and soft rim lighting.
- **Enemies**: shape language grouped by role (scout, brute, shaman, boss) so players parse threat instantly.
- **UI overlays**: semi-opaque panels, subtle blur, and accent borders for readability over art.
